Course 00 — DevOps Intro — Syllabus

A short, friendly orientation to what DevOps is, why it exists, and a roadmap of the courses ahead.

Duration~1 session (30–45 min)

Overview

A high-level orientation to the DevOps program. Students leave knowing what DevOps actually means (and what it doesn't), why the field exists, the cultural and technical pillars it rests on, and a roadmap of the courses ahead.

Learning outcomes

By the end of this course, a student will be able to:

  • Explain in their own words what DevOps is — culture first, automation second.
  • Describe the "wall" between Dev and Ops and why DevOps emerged to dismantle it.
  • Recall the CALMS pillars (Culture, Automation, Lean, Measurement, Sharing).
  • Sketch a basic CI/CD pipeline: commit → build → test → deploy.
  • Recognise the major categories of DevOps tooling and how Linux + open source underpin them.
